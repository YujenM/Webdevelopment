    Frequently Asked Questions

    Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It influences how we think, feel, and act in daily life. It also affects how we handle stress, relate to others, and make decisions. Good mental health is essential for overall well-being.
    Common signs include persistent sadness or irritability, withdrawal from friends and family, changes in eating or sleeping patterns, excessive worry or fear, lack of energy, and difficulty concentrating. Physical symptoms, such as headaches or stomach aches, can also be linked to mental health problems.
    Mental health disorders can result from a combination of factors including genetic predisposition, brain chemistry, trauma, and life experiences. Stress, substance abuse, and environmental factors may also contribute to the development of mental health issues.
    Listen without judgment, offer emotional support, and encourage them to seek professional help. Let them know they’re not alone, and be patient. You can also assist them in finding resources, like therapy or support groups, but avoid pushing them into anything they’re not ready for.
    Seek professional help if you or someone you know is experiencing persistent symptoms that interfere with daily life, such as overwhelming feelings of sadness, anxiety, or anger, difficulty functioning in work or social settings, or thoughts of self-harm. Reaching out to a mental health professional early can lead to better outcomes.
